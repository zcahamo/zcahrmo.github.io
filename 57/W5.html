Semi-direct product For groups \(K,Q\), form a group on the set \(K\times Q\). Let \(\theta :Q\to \text {Aut}(K)\) be a homomorphism, and define multiplication in \(K\rtimes _\theta Q\) by

\[ (k,q)(k',q')=\big (k\theta _q(k'),qq'\big )\in K\times Q \]

This is closed under multiplication. \(\theta _q\) is a homomorphism, so \(\theta _q(1)=1\), therefore

\[ (k,q)(1,1)=\big (k\theta _q(1),q\big )=(k,q) \]

Also, \(\theta _1\) is the identity element of \(\text {Aut}(K)\), so \(\theta _1(k)=k\), hence

\[ (1,1)(k,q)=\big (1\theta _1(k),q\big )=(k,q) \]

Therefore \((1,1)\) is the identity.

What about inverses? Find \(\tilde {k},\tilde {q}\) with

\(\seteqnumber{0}{}{3}\)

\begin{align*} (k,q)(\tilde {k},\tilde {q})&=(1,1)\\ \big (k\theta _q(\tilde {k}),q\tilde {q}\big )&=(1,1)\\ \end{align*} So \(\tilde {q}=q^{-1}\) and \(\theta _q(\tilde {k})=k^{-1}\). \(\theta _q\in \Aut (K)\), so some \(\theta _{q}^{-1}\) must exist such that

\[ \tilde {k}=\theta ^{-1}_q(k^{-1}) \]

To check, consider

\(\seteqnumber{0}{}{3}\)

\begin{align*} \big (\theta ^{-1}_q(k^{-1}),q^{-1})(k,q)&=\big (\theta ^{-1}_q(k^{-1})\theta _{q^{-1}}(k),1) \end{align*} \((\theta _q)^{-1}=\theta _{q^{-1}}\) since \(\theta \) is a homomorphism. Therefore

\[ \theta _{q^{-1}}(k^{-1})\theta _{q^{-1}}(k)=\theta _{q^{-1}}(k^{-1}k)=1 \]

Associativity is left as an exercise for the reader.
